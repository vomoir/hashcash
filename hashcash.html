The Mining Process
Miners work on validating "blocks" of recent transactions. Blocks are pages in the global ledger book tracking all transactions.
To make it hard for fraudsters to submit false verifications, Bitcoin makes it hard for everyone: to finish a block, you must complete a puzzle.
Miners pick an arbitrary number, called a 'nonce' (number used once). When the transactions and the nonce are put through a certain unpredictable, irreversible function, it spits out another number, called a 'hash', like 00881ff8db51cc89619ced803b0a5c7ebfcd488a.
The mining process will also specify the format of the hash in terms of how many leading zeros it must contain to be valid.
The first small-enough hash wins the block, and its 6.25 Bitcoin reward.
The block is added to the 'blockchain', the official record of every Bitcoin transaction ever.
(The first-ever Bitcoins were mined in the genesis block.)
